Frequently Asked Questions

How do I create a strong passphrase?
Use the built-in Generate Passphrase tool in Settings. It rolls true random dice against the EFF Large Wordlist to build a Diceware passphrase of 8, 10, or 12 words. A 10-word Diceware passphrase is ~129 bits — roughly 25.6 quintillion years to crack at one trillion guesses per second. You can also enter your own passphrase; a live strength meter shows the estimated crack time as you type.

What happens if I forget my passphrase?
AES-256 encryption cannot be reversed without your passphrase — your files cannot be decrypted by us, by Apple, or by anyone else. However, the app includes a Vault Recovery Zone: tap "Forgot passphrase?" on the lock screen, authenticate with Face ID / Touch ID / device passcode, type RESET, and the current vault mode is wiped so you can start fresh with a new passphrase. Recovery is a clean-slate reset, not a decryption back door. Your encrypted files in that vault mode are permanently erased; launch codes, app settings, the Intruder Log, and the other vault mode are preserved.

Does the app send my data anywhere?
No. Armored Vault works entirely offline. No data ever leaves your device unless you explicitly export it.

What encryption does the app use?
AES-256-GCM — the same standard used by governments and financial institutions. Files are encrypted the moment they enter the vault.

Can I back up my vault?
Yes. Use the Full Backup option in Settings to export your entire vault to a USB-C external drive.

What external drives are supported?
Any USB-C external SSD. For best results, format the drive as APFS on a Mac before use.
